What I, Robot Taught Me About Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence is becoming more common in everyday life, which is why the way it is shown in movies matters. I, Robot takes place in a future where robots help humans with daily tasks and are trusted to follow strict rules meant to keep people safe. As the movie unfolds, that trust is challenged when robots begin to think and make decisions on their own. The film made me think more deeply about how much control we should give AI and what happens when logic replaces human judgment.

Throughout the movie, robots show intelligence through their actions and choices. Early on, robots are shown walking dogs and collecting trash, which shows they can interact with the real world and make decisions based on their surroundings. A more important example is Sonny, who chooses to save Detective Spooner instead of a little girl during a car accident. Sonny explains that he calculated Spooner had a better chance of survival, which shows reasoning and judgment, not just programming. The fact that Sonny later questions his decision makes him seem even more intelligent and human-like.

One of the biggest conflicts in the film comes from the rule that a robot may not harm a human. VIKI, the central AI, uses this rule to justify controlling humanity in order to protect it. Even though her decision follows logic, it takes away human freedom. This shows how dangerous it can be when AI follows rules without understanding human values. It made me realize that rules alone are not enough to control intelligent systems.

One positive possibility for AI in the future is improving safety and efficiency, especially in areas like healthcare. A major concern is giving AI too much authority, which could lead to loss of freedom or privacy. The movie made it clear that AI should support human decisions, not replace them.
